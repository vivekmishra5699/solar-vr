JUPITER

- Largest planet, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.
- Strong magnetic field and powerful storms, including the Great Red Spot.
- Numerous moons, including the Galilean moons.
- Rapid rotation, oblate shape.
- Traps charged particles, creating intense radiation belts.
- Juno spacecraft provided detailed information about Jupiter's gravitational field and atmospheric composition.
- Jupiter's ring system is faint and mainly composed of small, dark particles.
- Great Red Spot, a massive storm, has been observed for over 350 years.
- Jupiter's rapid rotation results in an equatorial bulge.
- Galileo Galilei discovered Jupiter's four largest moons in 1610.
